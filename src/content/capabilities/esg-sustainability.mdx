ESG communications in 2026 operates under intense scrutiny. Stakeholders, regulators, and the media have become sophisticated at distinguishing genuine sustainability commitments from greenwashing, and the penalties for getting it wrong — reputational, regulatory, and commercial — are severe. Stromy helps organisations build sustainability narratives that are grounded in evidence, aligned with regulatory expectations, and resilient to challenge.

Our platform monitors the ESG media landscape across markets, tracking how sustainability issues are being covered, which companies are being praised or criticised, and where regulatory expectations are tightening. This intelligence informs communications strategy at every level — from annual sustainability report positioning to real-time response when a ESG-related issue attracts media attention. We also track the evolving taxonomy of sustainability standards and frameworks, helping clients understand which benchmarks their stakeholders actually care about and how their performance compares to peers.

The most effective ESG communications programmes are those that integrate intelligence into their reporting and stakeholder engagement cycles, rather than treating communications as a downstream function that packages decisions already made. We work with clients to build this integration, ensuring that sustainability communications are informed by data, aligned with strategy, and capable of withstanding the scrutiny they will inevitably attract.

## Key Outcomes

- ESG media landscape monitoring that tracks sustainability narratives, greenwashing accusations, and regulatory developments across markets
- Peer benchmarking against industry ESG communications standards, identifying gaps and opportunities for differentiation
- Regulatory horizon scanning for emerging sustainability disclosure requirements across jurisdictions, providing lead time for compliance
- Stakeholder perception analysis for ESG positioning, measuring how sustainability commitments are received by investors, media, communities, and employees
