Category A

Typographic Wordmarks

The brand name carries the full weight. No separate symbol — the typography IS the logo. Ideal for editorial brands like The Economist or Financial Times.
Concept 01

Score Wordmark

Wordmark + Score Lines

Two parallel lines beneath the name — like a musical score fragment. The oxide line with terminal node references a conductor's cue. Clean, editorial, directly connected to the maestro origin.

Concept 02

Fraunces Pure

Pure Typographic Wordmark

Maximum restraint — the serif face alone carries the brand. Like how The Economist or The New York Times let their masthead typography do all the work. Authority through letterform quality alone.

Concept 03

Baton Stroke

Wordmark + Conductor's Baton

A single diagonal stroke extending from the Y's terminus — the conductor's baton in motion. Minimal addition, maximum meaning. The oxide dot is the baton's tip catching a downbeat.

Wordmark + Distinctive Mark

The name stays primary, complemented by a small mark that can also stand alone as an icon. This is your stated preference — "stromy should be the main part, with a little something else."
Concept 04

A circuit/orchestration node preceding the name — two route lines diverge from a central hub (oxide core, forest ring). References workflow orchestration directly. The node works as a standalone favicon.

Concept 05

Concept 06

A fermata-like accent mark above the S — a musical notation symbol meaning "hold, the conductor decides." Subtle, cultured reference to the maestro origin. Works as a diacritical mark that makes the wordmark unique.
