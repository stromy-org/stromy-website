Section 02

Issues Identified

Issue 1 — SVG Text Elements (Critical)

All logo SVGs use <text> elements with font-family="'Fraunces'". If the font isn't loaded, the logo renders in Georgia or Times New Roman. Production logos must convert all text to <path> outlines. This is a blocking issue for any deployment.

Issue 2 — No Standalone Symbol

The "icon" variants are just the routing underline extracted from the wordmark — two thin lines and a small circle. They have almost no visual presence at small sizes and are not recognizable as an independent mark. There is no symbol that says "Stromy" without the word being present.

Issue 3 — Icon Legibility at Small Sizes

At 20px (favicon size), both the routed and classic icons are essentially invisible — thin lines and a tiny dot on a dark square. Compare to strong small-size marks: they use bolder geometry, higher contrast, and simpler shapes.

Issue 4 — Classic vs. Routed Identity Split

Having two parallel logo systems (routed + classic) dilutes the brand. The guidelines say "don't mix them in the same composition" but having both means the brand doesn't fully commit to either. Recommendation: pick one primary system and retire the other, or merge them into a single coherent family.

Section 04

Current Motif Family — Assessment

Strong

Tempo Ledger — Primary Signature

Two offset rules + registration block. Clean, recognizable, versatile. The best motif in the family. Keep and strengthen.

Good

Data Rail — Analytical Annotation

Vertical rail + checkpoint nodes + evidence stems. Effective for scorecards and sidebars. Could use slightly bolder stroke weights for better visibility.

Adequate

Switch Track — Process Language

Routing curve + junction. Directly echoes the logo concept. Works for workflow diagrams but quite thin. Upgrade: increase stroke weight and make the junction node more prominent.

Weakest

Echo Band — Rhythm & Transition

Three offset parallel lines with ghost echoes. Too subtle — at small scale it's nearly invisible. The ghost lines at 18-20% opacity barely register. Needs the most rework.

Logo System

Decision 1 — Symbol Mark

A: The Confluence — Two converging tracks + oxide node. S-shaped. Most connected to the routing concept.

B: The Ledger — Three offset registers + corner blocks. Most connected to the Tempo Ledger motif. Simplest, most readable at small sizes.

C: The Signal Path — Abstract S-curve with orchestration nodes. Most figurative, most "initial letter" feeling.

Which direction, or hybrid?

Decision 2 — Retire Classic?

Should we retire the classic line+dot system entirely, or keep it as a secondary option? Having two parallel logo systems dilutes the brand.

Decision 3 — Text to Paths

All logo SVGs need text converted to paths. This is non-negotiable for production but can happen after the design is finalized.

Motif Family

Decision 4 — Upgrade Strokes

Increase all motif stroke weights from 1.5px to 2-2.2px across the family? This gives more visual presence without changing the design language.

Decision 5 — Echo Band

Accept the upgraded Echo Band (35% ghost opacity, registration notch), or rethink this motif entirely? It's the weakest member of the family.

Decision 6 — Pattern Tile

The current pattern-tile.svg is quite subtle. Should we create a bolder variant alongside it for hero/cover contexts?
