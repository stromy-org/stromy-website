Motif Principles

The family is designed around one visual language: thin measured strokes, controlled offsets, clean node logic, and restrained rhythm. Each motif has a different job, but they all share the same tone and structural discipline.

Aligned

Shared stroke logic, spacing cadence, and color behavior across all four motifs.

Role-based

One motif for headers, one for data, one for process, one for transitions and section rhythm.

Scalable

Every motif is expected to work in micro annotations as well as large hero and divider contexts.

QA-first

Layouts must be reviewed visually for wrapping, overlap, cramped spacing, and unresolved compositions before presentation.
